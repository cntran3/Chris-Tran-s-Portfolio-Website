What is Media, Arts, and Sciences?

Media, Arts, and Sciences is an interdisciplinary field of study that integrates creative, technological, and scientific disciplines. This major prepares students for careers that blend art, technology, and media, focusing on areas such as digital media production, game design, interactive environments, and emerging media technologies.

Students in this program typically gain skills in:

  • Digital Media Production: Learning to create digital content such as video, audio, and animations.
  • Game Design and Development: Understanding the principles and techniques of designing and developing video games.
  • Interactive and Emerging Technologies: Exploring new media technologies, virtual reality, augmented reality, and other interactive platforms.
  • Art and Design: Developing artistic skills and understanding design principles to create visually compelling media.

Graduates can pursue various roles in industries such as film, gaming, advertising, and technology, where they can apply their creativity and technical skills to innovate and produce engaging media content.

For more information, you can check out the Media, Arts, and Sciences program page at ASU.
